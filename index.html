Project Overview

Users need a low cost and reliable way to travel without having to worry about their safety due to having an unfriendly or bad driver

My Role

Product Design, Research, Strategy, Wireframing, Prototyping.
I drew on several sources for my initial research. Online articles and videos. Rideshare has been around for years, but autonomous rideshare is very new. Waymo is the only operating autonomous rideshare system at this time.

I viewed videos of people using waymo as well as referencing written articles. The Waymo videos and articles were well curated, so I decided to look at peoples first responses to riding / driving in a Tesla with autopilot engaged.Uber and Lyft are the main competitors, their user experiences and app UIs follow close to the same pattern.  

To help delight and engage the user and be comforting, as well as follow Tesla’s current fun UI elements.  Which include several modes, such as Mars mode.

User and App Flow

The high level user and app flow are combined in this flow chart. I discovered that unlike a rideshare service with a driver, there would need to be a function if there were an emergency with the passenger or they remained in the vehicle after the drop off. User Journey

2) She goes to start her car, but it will not start and she has a morning meeting
3) She thinks about getting a rideshare but is hesitant because of experiences with bad and unfriendly drivers in the past
4) She has heard about the new autonomous rideshare service from Tesla and decides to give it a try
5) She downloads and opens the app and selects her destination
6) Car arrives right on time and she is pleased with her choice
7) She completes the trip and has a pleasant interaction with the app and was happy with her interaction

Wireframes

Mid-fi wireframes are my exploration of vehicle selection. The goals were to make the interactions delightful and usable at the same time. The initial vehicle selection screen used a carousel UI. I decided to go with a list and a visual element because all selections could be viewed at once.  Saving the user time and effort.

Select wireframes of home screen and evolution of vehicle selection screen.

I was initially going to use a carousel UI for the vehicle selection, but this would not allow the user to view all of the options at one glance. I iterated to a carousel display for the vehicle image display, but also included a text navigation to allow the user to view all options at once, and easily navigate between the cars. The carousel also allows for navigation, providing two ways for the users to make their selection.

Vehicle Selection Prototype 1.0

Vehicle Selection Flow

With this UI the users are not only provided with a seamless experience but also an intuitive one. They are able to easily select their preferred Robotaxi vehicle without any hassles. But what truly elevates this user experience is the motion design element I integrated. This isn't just for aesthetic appeal; it adds a touch of magic to the user journey. More importantly, this dynamic motion design informs the user about the specific type and appearance of the Tesla Robotaxi they are choosing. My aim was to merge functionality with delight, ensuring users are both informed and engaged throughout the vehicle selection process.
